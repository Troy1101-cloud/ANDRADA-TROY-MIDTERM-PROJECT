A GLIMPSE INTO MY LIFE

Childhood

I’m Troy Winter T. Andrada, born on April 1, 2007, sa Antipolo, Rizal. For the first four years, we lived there bago kami lumipat sa Cainta, Rizal. I’m the youngest sa anim naming magkakapatid five boys at isang girl. Since bata pa ako, curious na talaga ako sa paligid ko. I remember my first day sa Summerville when I was four years old medyo kinakabahan pero excited makilala ang classmates ko. My parents always encouraged me to do my best.

Laro laro kami ng siblings ko sa labas araw araw. I loved riding my bike around the neighborhood, playing hide and seek, at patintero. I also helped my mom sa kitchen. Holidays and birthdays were special kasi buo ang pamilya. I even won a small award noong first grade, and my teachers always said na curious at eager to learn ako. Childhood ko was full of fun, love, and simple joys that shaped who I am today.

Teenage Years

My teenage years were unique, especially during the pandemic. I experienced my first crush during high school, but because everything was online, we slowly lost connection. From that experience, natutunan ko ang patience at letting go. After that, I focused more on academics and chose the STEM strand in Senior High because I loved math and science.

Despite online learning challenges, Senior High was still fun. I ranked in the top 10 of my school and learned time management through online projects and activities. I bonded with classmates through games and study sessions. Those years taught me discipline, responsibility, and confidence preparing me for college life.

College

I started college at the University of the East Manila when I was only 17. I felt young compared to my classmates. At first, I wanted to become a doctor like my mom, but my dad encouraged me to take IT. I agreed and now I’m happy with my choice. College life is very different from Senior High. Subjects are more focused, and projects are mostly computer-related.

College taught me time management, teamwork, and self-discipline. I discovered new software tools and began enjoying programming, networking, and computer systems. Some days are tough, but every challenge teaches me patience and persistence. Overall, college life is challenging, fun, and truly transforming. 

ANDRADA, TROY WINTER T.
